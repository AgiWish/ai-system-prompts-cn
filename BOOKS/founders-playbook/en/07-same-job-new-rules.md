# Same job, new rules

In the AI area, the founder's job hasn't changed: find a real problem, build something that solves it, and scale it into a company that matters. What's changed is the path to get there. Across the four stages—Idea, MVP, Launch, and Scale—AI compresses quarters into weeks. Validation cycles that used to take months now take afternoons. A working prototype no longer requires a co-founder with the right stack; it requires a clear problem and a few focused sessions with a coding agent. Launch readiness compresses from a pre-launch scramble into a continuous workstream. And at scale, the operational weight that used to force early hires into firefighting roles can increasingly be handed off to AI, freeing your team to spend their attention on the judgment calls that become your moat. The bottlenecks are no longer what you can build, but what you choose to build.
